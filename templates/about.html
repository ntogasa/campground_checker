What is this?

Put simply, Campground Scavenger makes finding and reserving campgrounds easier. How does it do this? Well first, let's talk about the issue with recreation.gov.

What's the issue?

Recreation.gov is the online portal to all of the federally managed campgrounds in the United States. Thousands of campgrounds in popular locations like Zion, Yellowstone, and Yosemite can only be reserved through recreation.gov.

Unfortunately, recreation.gov is grossly inefficient.

To find and reserve a campground, you would need to first visit a park/forest/zone's homepage, and then click on each campground's individual webpage to find out whether or not there are any open campsites for your dates.

This means that if you want to check if ANY campsites are available in Sequoia National Forest, you could end up visiting ~30 different webpages.

So what can we do?

This inefficiency (which I believe is intentional design) is what Campground Scavenger is here to resolve. Campground Scavenger allows you to mass check as many campgrounds as you wish with the single click of a button, and tells you if there are any available campsites. It also provides links to each campground checked, so you can quickly and easily make reservations.

How to use

  1. Go to Campgrounds and search your desired parent zone (i.e. Kings Canyon National Park)
  2. Copy the ID numbers, and go to the Availability page
  3. Paste them in the input field, along with your target dates, and submit
  4. Wait for your results to appear!

I hope this makes it easier for you to plan your next outdoor excursion. Enjoy!


API

You can visit the API to access our campground database. Each campground object in the database includes a name, ID number, and parent zone name.
